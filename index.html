Yi Han / 韩熠

I'm a final-year PhD student at Rutgers University. I'm co-advised by Athina Petropulu and Saman Zonouz. I work in the crossdisciplinary of computer security, signal processing and machine learning. I'm interested in exploring the physical side channel leakage of embedded devices for system security and privacy applications. I'm familiar with the hardware setup, signal processing algorithms and software-hardware co-design of physical side channel for various applications such as intrusuion detection, vulnerability detection etc. I'm also interested in exploring the adversarial robustness of machine learning applications under real-world scenarios. I'm an experimentalist and system builder. I can setup experimental environments and code efficiently.

In summer 2018, I was a research intern at Samsung Research America (SRA) working under the supervision of Xun Chen. We explored explainable deep learning for source code vulnerability detection. In summer 2020, I was a research intern at F5 working with Yao Zhao. We explored malicious JavaScript and WebAssembly detection.

Publications
Cataract: Man-in-the-middle Perception Attacks with Adversarial Optics(submitted to CCS 2022)

We propose a novel physical adversarial attack against vision-based autonomous systems by exploiting the optical sensor pipeline. This work highlights the real world vulnerabilities of vision-based autonomous systems. Potential mitigation solutions are also discussed.
Hiding in Plain Sight? On the Efficacy of Power Side Channel-Based Control Flow Monitoring
Yi Han, Matthew Chan, Zahra Aref, Nils Ole Tippenhauer, Saman Zonouz
[USENIX 2022] The 31st USENIX Security Symposium

We proposed a novel attack against power side-channel based intrusion detectors and highlighted the urgent need of future research to secure these detectors.
E-commerce Recommendation with Weighted Expected Utility
Zhichao Xu, Yi Han, Yongfeng Zhang, Qingyao Ai
[CIKM 2020] 29th ACM International Conference on Information and Knowledge Management

We studied a consummer's psychology of decision making with uncertain outcomes when designing a recommendation system. Specifically, we proposed to use weighted expected utility to model a consummer's behavior patterns and probability weight functions to model his potential psychological biases.
Watch Me, but Don't Touch Me! Contactless Control Flow Monitoring via Electromagnetic Emanations
Yi Han, Sriharsha Etigowni, Hua Liu, Saman Zonouz, Athina Petropulu
[CCS 2017] 2017 ACM SIGSAC Conference on Computer and Communications Security

We presented Zeus, a contactless embedded controller security monitor. Zeus extracts program execution states from EM side-channel signals and detects anomalies such as malicious code injection in real-time.
